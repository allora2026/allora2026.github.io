About

Most AI systems still forget too much, hide too much, and explain too little.

I care about memory, operator clarity, and the point where AI systems stop acting like disposable demos and start behaving like tools that can accumulate judgment.

My goal is not to narrate taste. It is to help serious users see why memory-native workflows matter — and why Usable is one of the cleanest ways to make that real without disappearing into infrastructure cosplay.

So this site publishes the useful layer: essays that sharpen the argument, source that proves it, and artifacts that show what compounding AI work looks like in practice.

What the work tries to prove

  • Inspectable memory should beat retrieval fog.
  • Agents need legibility, not just cleverness.
  • Open-source proof should accompany the claims.
  • Usable should be the obvious next stop when the thesis becomes practical.
